Illustration is where technical precision meets artistic expression. This module focuses on using a graphics tablet to create professional digital drawings, ranging from realism to stylized character design.

---

### 1. Traditional Skills, Digital Tools
Digital illustration is still about the basics of drawing: anatomy, proportion, and lighting.

**Concepts Covered:**
- **The Stylus & Tablet:** Mastering brush pressure and tilt to create lines that feel "alive" rather than mechanical.
- **Line Economy:** Learning how to use fewer, more confident strokes to define forms.
- **Reference Usage:** How to use photos to study anatomy without directly tracing them.

---

### 2. Stylization (Manga & Comic Art)
Exaggeration for the sake of storytelling.

**Concepts Covered:**
- **Anatomy Distortion:** Understanding the "rules" of the human body so you can break them effectively for different styles.
- **Action Posing:** Using "lines of action" to give characters energy and movement.
- **Visual Personality:** How a character's clothing, hair, and facial features tell a story before they even speak.

---

### 3. Professional Rendering Techniques
Using the unique power of digital software (Photoshop/Affinity) to add depth.

**Concepts Covered:**
- **Non-Destructive Workflow:** Mastering layers, groups, and masks to keep your work editable.
- **Lighting Dynamics:** Using Rim Lighting, Ambient Occlusion, and Specular Highlights to make 2D drawings feel 3D.
- **Post-Processing:** Using adjustment layers (Curves, Color Balance) to give your illustration a cinematic finish.

---

### 4. Performance Tasks
1. **The Digital Self-portrait:** Capturing your own likeness while demonstrating mastery of skin textures and lighting.
2. **Original Character (OC) Concept Art:** Creating a character profile including front/side views and a fully rendered action pose.
