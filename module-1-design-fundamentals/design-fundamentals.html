Core Concepts

Before we touch the software, we must understand the "why" and "how" of visual arrangement. In this module, we explore the self and memory through design.

Art vs. Design

Purpose & Problem Solving

Art: The "Look at Me"

Art is often subjective. It's about expression, emotion, and asking questions. The goal is the artist's personal vision.

Design: The "Follow Me"

Design is objective. It's about problem-solving, utility, and providing answers. The goal is to guide the user to a solution.

"Art is about the window; Design is about the door."

Elements of Design

The Ingredients of a Visual Dish

Line, Shape, Color, Texture, Space, Form, Value

What are they?

Elements are the tangible things that make up any design. If you can see it, it's made of these components.

  • Line: The start of everything.
  • Shape: 2D boundaries.
  • Color: The mood setter.
  • Texture: Surface quality.

Element 1: Line

The Path of a Moving Point

Types of Lines

  • Horizontal: Calm, stability, and rest.
  • Vertical: Strength, power, and growth.
  • Diagonal: Movement, action, and tension.
  • Curved: Fluidity, grace, and organic feel.

Element 2: Shape

2D Boundaries & Enclosures

Geometric vs. Organic

Geometric: Precise, mathematical shapes (circles, squares). They feel man-made and structured.

Organic: Natural, free-form, and unpredictable (leaves, puddles). They feel biological and soft.

Principles of Design

The Recipe for Success

Balance, Contrast, Hierarchy, Rhythm, Pattern, Emphasis, Unity

How to use them?

Principles are the rules. They describe how to arrange the elements to create a message that works.

  • Contrast: Make things different.
  • Hierarchy: Order of importance.
  • Balance: Visual weight.
  • Unity: The "Whole" feeling.

Principle 1: Balance

Visual Equilibrium

Symmetrical vs. Asymmetrical

Symmetrical: Equal weight on both sides. Feels formal, stable, and organized.

Asymmetrical: Different weights that still balance each other out. Feels dynamic, modern, and interesting.

Principle 2: Contrast

The Power of Difference

Creating Focus

Contrast occurs when two elements are different. The greater the difference, the more they stand out.

  • Big vs. Small (Size)
  • Black vs. White (Value)
  • Rough vs. Smooth (Texture)
