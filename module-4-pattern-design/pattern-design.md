### 1. Organic vs. Geometric Logic
Every pattern follows a set of rules.

**Concepts Covered:**
- **Natural Logic:** Patterns found in trees, skins, and water. These rely on "Chaos controlled by order".
- **Geometric Logic:** Patterns built from strict shapes and grids. These rely on mathematical symmetry and repetition.
- **The Mood of Patterns:** Soft/Natural patterns evoke comfort, while Sharp/Geometric patterns evoke modernism and technology.

---

### 2. The Technical Art of Tiling
A professional pattern must be "seamless".

**Concepts Covered:**
- **The Repeat Unit (Tile):** Identifying the smallest section of a design that contains all the elements needed to repeat the pattern.
- **The Offset Technique:** A digital technique in Photoshop or Illustrator to move the edges of a design to the center, ensuring the repeat is perfect.
- **Vignetting and Balance:** How to avoid "dark spots" or "bright spots" that become obvious when a pattern is repeated across a large area.

---

### 3. Application and Impact
Patterns are rarely the "star" but they set the "tone" for everything else.

**Concepts Covered:**
- **Branding Applications:** Using subtle patterns to give a brand a premium feel.
- **Mockups:** Visualizing how a 2D pattern will look when applied to a 3D object like a phone case or a cushion.
- **Tessellations:** Exploring the mathematical art of M.C. Escher, where shapes fit together perfectly without gaps.

---

### 4. Performance Tasks
1. **Dynamic Nature Tile:** Design a seamless pattern inspired by a biological texture.
2. **Modern Geometric Tile:** Construct a precise geometric repeat.
3. **Wallpaper Showcase:** Present your patterns applied to a simulated digital desktop or mobile device.
