# Role: Digital Illustrator & Line Artist
# Task: Line Art Composition — Theme: "School"

## Project Context
In this assignment, you will explore the power of **Line** as a fundamental element of design. You are tasked with creating a digital illustration using **Affinity** (Designer or Photo), focusing strictly on line-based representation. By limiting your tools, you will learn how to communicate form, depth, and texture through varied line weights and strokes.

---

## The Brief
Create a digital line art illustration based on the theme **"School"**. This can be anything related to the academic environment: a classroom scene, a desktop with supplies, architectural details of a school building, or even a conceptual representation of learning.

### Technical Constraints
*   **Software:** Affinity (Designer or Photo).
*   **Tool:** Strictly the **Paint Brush Tool**. 
*   **Style:** Line art only. No solid fills, no gradients, and no complex effects. Use lines to create shapes and shading (e.g., hatching or cross-hatching).
*   **Canvas Size:** A4 or Letter (300 DPI).

---

## Core Requirements
*   **Theme Interpretation:** The artwork must clearly relate to the theme of "School".
*   **Line Quality:** Demonstrate variety in line weight (tapering, thickness) to show depth and focus.
*   **Composition:** Use the entire canvas effectively. Apply principles like the Rule of Thirds or Leading Lines.
*   **Clarity:** The subject should be recognizable through line work alone.

---

## Output Format
Please submit:
1.  **The Digital Illustration:** Exported as a high-resolution PNG or PDF.
2.  **Affinity Source File:** (.afdesign or .afphoto).
3.  **Design Rationale (100-150 words):** 
    *   Describe your interpretation of the "School" theme.
    *   Explain how you used line variety to create depth or emphasis in your work.

---

## 💡 Teacher's Tip: The "Single Tool" Challenge
Using only the Paint Brush tool might feel restrictive, but it's a great way to master **hand-eye coordination** and **pressure sensitivity**. 
*   If you have a drawing tablet, use pressure to vary your line width.
*   If using a mouse, experiment with the "Stabilizer" settings in Affinity to get smoother, cleaner strokes.

---

## Evaluation Criteria (100 Points)
*   **Theme Relevance (30 pts):** How well does the art represent "School"?
*   **Line Mastery (40 pts):** Variety in line weight, smoothness of strokes, and technical control.
*   **Composition (20 pts):** Effective use of the canvas and visual balance.
*   **Technical Compliance (10 pts):** Strictly used the Paint Brush tool and met file requirements.
