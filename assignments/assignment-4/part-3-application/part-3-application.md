## Part 3: Applying to a Project (Format Choice)
Apply your 60-30-10 palette from Part 2 to a mock design. You must choose **one** format to learn its specific color constraints:

### Option A: Website Design (RGB)
*   **Focus:** Vibrant, digital-first colors.
*   **Constraint:** Colors must be digital-first. They should remain vibrant on high-resolution screens.
*   **Goal:** Maximize energy and screen interaction.
*   **What to Design:** A single-page **Website Landing Page** layout that includes at minimum:
    *   A **hero section** with a background color, headline, and sub-headline.
    *   A **navigation bar** with menu links.
    *   A prominent **Call-to-Action (CTA) button** (e.g., "Get Started", "Learn More").
    *   A **footer** section.
*   **Key Considerations:**
    *   Use your **Primary (60%) color** for the large background areas and whitespace.
    *   Apply your **Secondary (30%) color** to the navigation bar, footer, and any content cards or sections.
    *   Reserve your **Accent (10%) color** exclusively for the CTA button, links, and interactive highlights.
    *   Ensure text is highly readable—dark text on light backgrounds, or light text on dark backgrounds.
    *   Use **Affinity Designer** to create your layout. You can also use **Affinity Photo** for image-based hero sections.

### Option B: Poster Design (CMYK)
*   **Focus:** Print-ready professional signage.
*   **Constraint:** Check if your digital colors are "out of gamut" (printable). Learn that neon greens/bright blues might dull when converted to CMYK.
*   **Goal:** Ensure color accuracy from screen to paper.
*   **What to Design:** An **18" × 24" Event Poster** layout that includes at minimum:
    *   A **large background area** dominated by your primary color.
    *   An **event title block** using your secondary color.
    *   A **date/time/venue highlight box** using your accent color.
    *   Supporting text for event details.
*   **Key Considerations:**
    *   Convert your RGB hex codes to **CMYK values** using a color converter tool (see resources below).
    *   Watch for **out-of-gamut warnings**—some bright RGB colors (neon greens, electric blues, hot pinks) cannot be accurately reproduced in CMYK printing.
    *   For rich blacks in print, use a CMYK blend (e.g., C:40 M:40 Y:40 K:100) instead of just K:100.
    *   Always note both your original RGB hex code AND the converted CMYK values in your submission.
    *   Use **Affinity Publisher** or **Affinity Designer** — both have built-in CMYK document color space support.

## Step-by-Step Guide

### For Option A (Website Landing Page — RGB):
1.  **Open Affinity Designer** and create a new document with an RGB color profile.
2.  **Set your canvas size** to a standard web resolution (e.g., 1440×900 px for desktop).
3.  **Block out the layout** in grayscale first—draw rectangles for the hero section, navbar, CTA, and footer.
4.  **Apply your 60-30-10 palette** from Part 2:
    *   Fill the hero background with your **Primary (60%)** color.
    *   Color the navbar and footer with your **Secondary (30%)** color.
    *   Style the CTA button with your **Accent (10%)** color.
5.  **Add placeholder text** using the Artistic Text or Frame Text tool for the headline, sub-headline, and menu items.
6.  **Check readability** — zoom out and squint. Can you still identify the CTA button? Is the text readable?
7.  **Export as JPEG or PNG** (high resolution, at least 1440px wide).

### For Option B (Event Poster — CMYK):
1.  **Open Affinity Publisher** (or Affinity Designer) and create a new document with a **CMYK/8** color profile.
2.  **Set your canvas size** to 18" × 24" at 300 DPI (for print quality).
3.  **Block out the layout** — sketch the title area, background, and detail boxes.
4.  **Apply your 60-30-10 palette** from Part 2:
    *   Fill the large background with your **Primary (60%)** color.
    *   Color the event title block with your **Secondary (30%)** color.
    *   Highlight the date/venue box with your **Accent (10%)** color.
5.  **Use Affinity's built-in color picker** to enter your HEX values — the software will automatically convert them to CMYK in the document's color space. Check for any gamut warnings in the color panel.
6.  **Check for gamut issues** — in Affinity, look for the gamut warning indicator in the color picker. If any color is out of gamut, note how you adjusted it.
7.  **Export as PDF** (use File → Export → PDF, select **PDF/X-1a** or **PDF/X-4** preset for print-ready CMYK output).

## 🌐 Real-World Websites Using the 60-30-10 Rule

Study these live websites to see how professional brands apply the rule. Notice how they balance color proportions:

| Brand | 60% (Dominant) | 30% (Secondary) | 10% (Accent) |
|-------|----------------|------------------|---------------|
| **Apple.com** | White/Light Gray backgrounds | Product images and dark text blocks | Blue "Buy" CTA buttons |
| **Canva.com** | White page backgrounds | Blue headers, navigation, and cards | Purple accent buttons and badges |
| **Slack.com** | Muted purple/neutral backgrounds | Vibrant illustration panels | Bold CTA buttons |
| **Google Workspace** | White backgrounds | Blue UI elements | Bright blue/green action buttons |

Please submit:
1.  **Mock Design:** A simple layout (Website landing page or Poster) applying the 60-30-10 rule.
2.  **Color Specification Table:** List each color used with its Hex code, role (60/30/10), and where it's applied.
3.  **Design Rationale (150 words):** Why did these colors work in the original scene, and how did you adjust them for your chosen output (RGB vs CMYK)?
