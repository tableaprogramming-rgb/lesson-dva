01. Diagnostic Analysis

Hierarchy & Contrast

  • Typographic Hierarchy: Identify if the focal point is missing or if elements are competing for attention.
  • Contrast & Legibility: Evaluate the color choices and text readability from a distance.

Noise & Technicals

  • Visual Noise: Note any unnecessary clutter, images, or "vibrating" colors.
  • Technical Errors: Look for poor kerning (letter spacing), leading (line spacing), or alignment issues.

02. Improvement Strategy

Structure & Flow

  • Hierarchy: Explain how to use scale and weight to create a clear focal point.
  • Composition: Explain how to use a Grid System and Negative Space to reduce clutter.

Aesthetics & Utility

  • Typography: Suggest specific font styles (e.g., Sans-Serif) for better street-level readability.
  • Color Theory: Recommend a high-contrast color palette (e.g., 60-30-10 rule).

03. Reference Case Studies

Case Study 01

The "Text-Heavy" Restaurant Sign

Scenario: A local eatery listing its entire menu, phone number, and slogans on one 4x4 sign.

What makes it "Bad"

  • Information Overload: Passers-by cannot process 50 words in 3 seconds.
  • Poor Hierarchy: Business name competes with side dish listings.

The Digital Surgery

  • The 5-Second Rule: Strip everything except the core name and "hero" visual.
  • Execution: Use Bold Sans-Serif for the name; relocate phone to secondary position.
Case Study 02

The "Low-Contrast" Service Banner

Scenario: A car wash or repair shop using a yellow background with white or light-green text.

What makes it "Bad"

  • Value Conflict: Similar values cause colors to "vibrate" and become invisible in sunlight.
  • Readability: Causes eye strain and fails basic legibility tests.

The Digital Surgery

  • Black & Gold Rule: Swap text to high-contrast dark navy or black.
  • Execution: Use Eyedropper to sample environmental shadows for perfectly legible text.
Case Study 03

The "Clipart Chaos" Pet Shop

Scenario: Small pet shop with 10 low-res pictures scattered randomly across the storefront.

What makes it "Bad"

  • Visual Noise: Total lack of "White Space" creates sensory overload.
  • Scaling Issues: Pixelated raster images look unprofessional and blurry.

The Digital Surgery

  • Vectorization: Replace 10 photos with one clean, high-quality vector icon.
  • Execution: Use Negative Space to frame the name, creating instant brand recognition.
