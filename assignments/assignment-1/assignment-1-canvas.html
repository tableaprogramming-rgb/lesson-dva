Senior Digital Design Consultant

Assignment 1: Visual Audit and Redesign Strategy

Project Context

I am currently studying Digital Visual Design. I have a screenshot from Google Street View of a real-world design (signage/billboard/storefront) that is visually ineffective. I need to perform "Digital Surgery" to fix it.

Part 1: Diagnostic Analysis

Analyze the provided design based on the following "Bad Design" criteria:

  • Typographic Hierarchy: Identify if the focal point is missing or if elements are competing for attention.
  • Contrast & Legibility: Evaluate the color choices and text readability from a distance.
  • Visual Noise: Note any unnecessary clutter, images, or "vibrating" colors.
  • Technical Errors: Look for poor kerning (letter spacing), leading (line spacing), or alignment issues.

Part 2: Improvement Strategy (The Redesign)

Provide a step-by-step technical plan to improve the design using professional tools (Photoshop/Illustrator):

1. Hierarchy Explain how to use scale and weight to create a clear focal point.
2. Typography Suggest specific font styles (e.g., Sans-Serif) for better street-level readability.
3. Color Theory Recommend a high-contrast color palette (e.g., 60-30-10 rule).
4. Composition Explain how to use a Grid System and Negative Space to reduce clutter.

Output Format

Please provide the response in two clear sections:

  1. The Diagnosis (Identifying the "Bad" elements).
  2. The Digital Surgery (Step-by-step technical improvements).

📚 Reference Case Studies

Example 1: The "Text-Heavy" Restaurant Sign

The Scenario: A local eatery that lists its entire menu, phone number, website, and three different slogans on one 4x4 storefront sign.

What makes it "Bad":
  • Information Overload: Drivers cannot process 50 words in 3 seconds.
  • Poor Hierarchy: Restaurant name is the same size as secondary details.
How to Improve it:
  • The "5-Second Rule": Strip everything except the Name and one high-quality visual.
  • The Digital Fix: Use Bold Sans-Serif and clear sizing differences.

Example 2: The "Low-Contrast" Service Banner

The Scenario: A car wash using a yellow background with white or light-green text.

What makes it "Bad":
  • Value Contrast: Colors "vibrate" and become invisible under direct sunlight.
  • Readability: Causes eye strain and fails the "squint test."
How to Improve it:
  • The "Black & Gold" Rule: Change the text to black or dark navy blue.
  • The Digital Fix: Use the Eyedropper Tool to sample high-contrast colors.

Example 3: The "Clipart Chaos" Pet Shop

The Scenario: A pet grooming shop with 10 different low-res pictures scattered randomly across the window.

What makes it "Bad":
  • Visual Noise: No "white space"; eye is overwhelmed.
  • Scaling Issues: Images look pixelated and low-quality.
How to Improve it:
  • Vectorization: Use high-quality icons instead of multiple low-res photos.
  • The Digital Fix: Use Negative Space to frame the name clearly.

💡 Teacher's Tip: The "Squint Test"

Tell your students to look at their Street View screenshot and squint their eyes.

  • If the sign turns into a blurry blob where nothing stands out, it has bad hierarchy.
  • If the text disappears entirely, it has bad contrast.
