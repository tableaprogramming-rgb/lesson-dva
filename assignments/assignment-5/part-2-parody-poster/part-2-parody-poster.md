## Part 2: Parody Poster

### The Brief
Create a parody of a **famous movie, game, book, or media poster**. Your goal is to mimic the original style perfectly while subverting the message with humor, irony, or commentary.

### Core Requirements
* **Instant Recognition:** The original poster must be immediately identifiable.
* **Perfect Mimicry:** Match the original's:
  * Color palette (exact or very close)
  * Typography (same fonts or convincingly similar)
  * Layout and compositional structure
  * Photography/illustration style
  * Graphic elements (borders, textures, overlays)
* **Clever Subversion:** The twist should be:
  * Unexpected and memorable
  * Funny, thought-provoking, or culturally relevant
  * Clear in its commentary or humor
* **Same Dimensions:** 18"×24" (or match the original poster size).

---

## The Three-Step Parody Process

### Step 1: Analyze
Study the original poster's visual DNA with forensic precision:

**Color Palette**
* Which colors define the style?
* Complementary scheme? Analogous? Monochromatic?
* How do colors create mood or association?

**Typography**
* Serif or sans-serif? Which specific fonts?
* What weights (light, medium, bold, extra-bold)?
* How is text positioned?

**Layout & Grid**
* Rule of thirds? Centered? Asymmetrical?
* Where is negative space used?
* How do elements interact?

**Photography/Imagery Style**
* High contrast or soft? Color-saturated or muted?
* Close-up or wide shot?
* What mood do images convey?

**Graphic Elements**
* Icons, lines, borders, textures, overlays?
* How are they integrated?

**Overall Composition**
* Symmetrical or asymmetrical balance?
* What is the focal point strategy?

### Step 2: Mimic
Recreate the visual DNA convincingly. **The parody only works if viewers instantly recognize the original.**

Get these details right:
* Match the color palette exactly (use a color picker on the original)
* Use the same fonts or very close alternatives
* Mirror the compositional structure precisely
* Replicate the photography or imagery style
* Maintain identical typography hierarchy and sizing
* Use identical graphic elements and decorative features

This step is about **being a faithful copycat**. Precision matters.

### Step 3: Subvert
Now comes the creativity. Change the message to surprise, amuse, or provoke:

* Keep the style identical
* Swap the copy, subject, or context
* The familiar form + unexpected content = recognition + impact

The twist should be:
* **Clever:** Not obvious, but satisfying when you "get it"
* **Memorable:** Something viewers will want to share or discuss
* **Appropriate:** Not mean-spirited; it's fair game

## Types of Parody Humor

### Situational Humor
* Movie poster becomes anti-war poster
* Luxury brand becomes social justice statement
* Familiar context, unexpected twist

### Visual Puns
* Clever wordplay combined with imagery
* Image mashups (two recognizable icons combined unexpectedly)
* Hidden meanings revealed on second look

### Exaggeration
* Pushing design elements to absurd levels
* Over-saturation of color, size, or pattern
* Contrast between serious original and ridiculous parody = humor

### Unexpected Twist
* Viewer expects one thing, experiences another
* Familiar celebrity or character in unfamiliar context
* Serious brand's style applied to something trivial (or vice versa)

---

## Timing is Everything: The "Aha" Moment

In poster design, humor relies on instant recognition. The best parodies have:

1. **Instant Recognition (0 seconds):** Viewers immediately recognize the original style
2. **A Moment of Confusion (1-2 seconds):** Wait... why does this look familiar?
3. **The Payoff (2-3 seconds):** Understanding the twist
4. **The Laugh/Realization (3+ seconds):** Appreciation and desire to share

## Example Parodies

### Example 1: "Star Wars" → "Stair Wars"
**Original Reference:** Classic Star Wars poster (bold yellow title, starfield background, dramatic character poses)

**The Twist:** Replace lightsabers with brooms; replace characters with janitors in dramatic poses

**Typography:** Identical bold yellow title font: "STAIR WARS: A Clean Hope"

**Tagline:** "In a building far, far away... the janitors fight for spotless floors."

**Why it Works:**
* **Instant Recognition:** Star Wars fans immediately recognize the style
* **Perfect Mimicry:** Colors, fonts, and composition match the original
* **Subversion:** The humor lands because serious style clashes with mundane subject

---

### Example 2: "Jaws" → "Paws"
**Original Style:** Dark ocean, menacing shark, dramatic red color accent

**Parody Concept:** Replace shark with rubber duck; change to comedic pet movie

**Typography:** Same distressed font style, but "PAWS" instead of "JAWS"

**Why it Works:**
* Iconic original that's instantly recognizable
* Simple concept swap (predator → pet)
* Visual humor is immediate

## The Design Process

### Step 1: Original Selection
Choose a famous poster that:
* Is visually distinctive and iconic
* Has a clear, recognizable style
* Offers opportunities for clever subversion

**Good choices:**
* Movie posters (Marvel, Sci-Fi, Horror)
* Video game promotional art
* Album cover art
* Famous political or activism posters
* Corporate/brand campaigns

### Step 2: Analysis & Documentation
* Gather high-res image of original poster
* Analyze color palette (use color picker)
* Document exact fonts (use font-matching tools)
* Screenshot layout grid structure
* Note any special effects (shadows, glows, textures)

### Step 3: Sketch & Concept
* Sketch 5-10 parody concepts on paper
* What message or subject will you substitute?
* How can you subvert it cleverly?
* What's the "aha" moment?
* Choose the strongest concept

### Step 4: Digital Recreation
* Create 18"×24" document at 300 DPI
* Match background color exactly
* Replicate any gradient or texture
* Set up typography at exact sizes
* Build digital mockup matching original

### Step 5: Insert New Content
* Photograph or source new images matching parody concept
* Match original's lighting and contrast style
* Update headlines (keep font/size/color identical)
* Replicate shadows/effects on new elements
* Maintain visual balance

### Step 6: Feedback & Polish
* Show to friends without explanation
* Do they recognize the original immediately?
* Do they understand the parody?
* Does the humor land?
* Polish and refine based on feedback

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

**❌ Style Doesn't Match Closely Enough**
* Viewers don't recognize the original
* Parody falls flat

**❌ Subversion is Too Subtle**
* Viewers don't understand the twist
* Clever idea is lost

**❌ Subversion is Too Obvious**
* No "aha" moment—just predictable
* Feels forced rather than clever

**❌ Execution is Shoddy**
* Text doesn't match font, colors are off
* Design looks amateurish

**❌ Parody is Mean-Spirited**
* Makes fun of person/group inappropriately
* Viewers feel uncomfortable rather than amused

## Technical Specifications

### Poster Dimensions
* **Minimum Size:** 18"×24" (or match original poster size)
* **Alternative Sizes:** 24"×36" or 27"×40" (movie poster standard)

### File Setup
* **Resolution:** 300 DPI (for print quality)
* **Color Mode:** CMYK (if printing); RGB (if digital display only)
* **Bleed Area:** Add 0.25" around all edges for professional printing
* **File Format:** PDF (preferred), PNG (high-res), or TIFF

---

## Output Format

Please submit:

1. **Digital Parody File:** High-resolution PDF or PNG (300 DPI, 18"×24" or larger)
2. **Source File:** The original design file (.ai, .psd, .fig, or .afdesign)
3. **Original Reference Image:** Screenshot of the original poster you're parodying
4. **Parody Rationale (150-200 words):**
   * What original poster did you parody and why?
   * What message or twist did you introduce?
   * How did you replicate the original's visual DNA?
   * What makes the parody clever or memorable?
   * How did you ensure viewers would recognize both original and subversion?

## 💡 Teacher's Tip: The Recognition Test

Show your parody to a friend **without explaining it**:
* Do they recognize the original within 3 seconds? ✓
* Do they understand the parody/twist? ✓
* Do they laugh, appreciate it, or want to share it? ✓

If yes to all three, your parody is working.
