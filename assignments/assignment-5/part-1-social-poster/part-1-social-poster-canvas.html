Visual Activist & Creative Director

Part 1: Social Issue Poster — Design as Activism

Project Context

You are now a Visual Activist. In this part, you will design a poster that advocates for a social issue you care about. Your goal is to create a design that provokes thought, evokes emotion, and calls viewers to action.

The Brief

Design an 18"×24" (or larger) poster that raises awareness about a social, environmental, or cultural issue you are passionate about.

Core Requirements

  • Message Clarity: The issue must be understood within 3 seconds of viewing.
  • Emotional Impact: The design should evoke a specific emotion (urgency, hope, empathy, outrage).
  • Call to Action: Include a clear directive (Vote / Donate / Learn More / Take Action).
  • Visual Hierarchy: Use the 70-20-10 rule (hero 70%, supporting 20%, fine print 10%).
  • Symbolic Minimalism: Use ONE powerful symbol or image (not multiple competing visuals).
  • Typography at Scale: Minimum 72pt for headlines; readable from 15+ feet away.
  • Negative Space: Strategic use of empty space to create focus and sophistication.

Design Considerations

  • Color Psychology: Choose 1-3 colors that reinforce your message emotionally.
  • Composition: Symmetrical (formal, stable) or asymmetrical (dynamic, modern) balance?
  • Focal Point: What is the FIRST thing viewers see? Make it impossible to miss.
  • Distance Testing: Print or display on a large screen and view from 20 feet away. Does it work?

Examples of Social Issues

  • Climate change & environmental sustainability
  • Mental health awareness
  • Education access & equality
  • Digital privacy & data rights
  • Social justice & human rights
  • Public health campaigns

Design Process: From Concept to Execution

Step 1: Concept Development

  • What is your core message?
  • Who is your target audience?
  • What emotion should it evoke?
  • What action should it inspire?

Step 2: Research & Sketching

  • Study successful precedents (5-10 posters minimum)
  • Rough out 5-10 layout ideas by hand
  • Test different hierarchy approaches
  • Explore color combinations

Step 3: Refinement

  • Choose the strongest concept
  • Build it digitally
  • Test colors, fonts, and hierarchy
  • Get feedback from peers

Step 4: Distance Testing

  • View from 20 feet away
  • The 3-Second Rule: Can someone understand the message in 3 seconds?
  • Check for legibility issues
  • Revise as needed

Color Psychology

💡 Teacher's Tips

The "20-Foot Test"

  • Can you identify the focal point instantly?
  • Can you read the main message?
  • Does the poster command attention?

The "Squint Test"

Squint while looking at your poster: Does ONE element remain dominant? Good hierarchy. Does everything blur equally? Revise.

The "Three-Second Rule"

Show your poster for 3 seconds, then hide it. Can they tell you what it's about? If not, simplify.

Submission Format

  1. Digital Poster File: High-resolution PDF or PNG (300 DPI, 18"×24" or larger)
  2. Source File: The original design file (.ai, .psd, .fig, or .afdesign)
  3. Design Rationale (150-200 words):
    • What social issue did you choose and why?
    • What message did you want to communicate?
    • How did you use color, typography, and imagery to create emotional impact?
    • What is your call to action?
    • How does your design command attention in 3 seconds?
