# Role: Graphic Designer & Illustrator
# Task: Geometric Composition — Line & Shape Integration

## Project Context
In the previous assignment, you explored the power of **Line** to create form and depth. Now, you will transition into the world of **Shape**. In design, shapes are the building blocks of visual communication. By combining the precision of lines with the weight of geometric and organic shapes, you will create a cohesive and balanced composition using **Affinity Designer**.

---

## The Brief
Design a balanced composition that explores the relationship between **Line** and **Shape**. Your goal is to represent a theme (choose one from the options below) using only geometric shapes (circles, squares, triangles, etc.) and line work.

### Theme Options (Choose One)
1.  **Urban Jungle:** The intersection of architecture and nature.
2.  **Celestial Voyage:** Concepts of space, planets, and cosmic energy.
3.  **Mechanical Life:** Biological organisms reimagined as machines.

### Technical Constraints
*   **Software:** Affinity Designer.
*   **Tools:** 
    *   **Shape Tools:** (Rectangle, Ellipse, Polygon, etc.)
    *   **Pen Tool:** For custom shape creation and precise line work.
    *   **Stroke & Fill:** You must utilize both Outlines (Lines) and Solid Fills (Shapes).
*   **Color Palette:** Limited to 3-4 colors (including Black/White).
*   **Canvas Size:** 1080px x 1080px (Square).

---

## Core Requirements
*   **Integration:** Lines and shapes must work together (e.g., lines connecting shapes, shapes used as anchors for lines).
*   **Balance:** Demonstrate a clear sense of visual balance (Symmetrical or Asymmetrical).
*   **Complexity:** At least 5 distinct shapes and varied line weights must be present.
*   **Negative Space:** Utilize the background intentionally to define your forms.

---

## Output Format
Please submit:
1.  **The Digital Composition:** Exported as a high-resolution PNG or JPG.
2.  **Affinity Designer Source File:** (.afdesign).
3.  **Design Rationale (100-200 words):** 
    *   Identify your chosen theme.
    *   Explain how you integrated line and shape to communicate your concept.
    *   Describe your color choices and how they contribute to the mood.

---

## 💡 Teacher's Tip: "Form follows Function"
When building your composition, think about the **function** of each element. 
*   **Shapes** are stable and provide "weight" to your design.
*   **Lines** are dynamic and provide "direction" or movement.
*   Try overlapping shapes and using different stroke alignments (Inner, Center, Outer) to create unique visual intersections.

---

## Evaluation Criteria (100 Points)
*   **Technical Execution (30 pts):** Clean paths, proper use of shape tools and Pen tool.
*   **Concept & Integration (30 pts):** How effectively line and shape are fused to represent the theme.
*   **Composition & Balance (30 pts):** Overall visual impact and use of design principles.
*   **Technical Compliance (10 pts):** Adherence to color palette and tool constraints.
