Module Overview

Typography is where design meets language. This module shifts from working with visual shapes and images to working with letterforms—the most precise, powerful, and nuanced tool in a designer's arsenal.

Key Understanding: Typography is not simply choosing a font. It's a comprehensive system that includes font selection, spacing, alignment, sizing, weight, color, and hierarchy.

What is Typography?

Many beginners think: "Typography = picking a font."

Reality: Typography is the entire discipline of making text communicate effectively. It includes much more than font choice.

Three Responsibilities of Typography

1. Legibility

Can the reader see and distinguish individual letters?

Determined by:

  • Typeface design (serifs, stroke weight, x-height)
  • Font size relative to viewing distance
  • Color contrast against the background
  • Distinguishability of tricky pairs (l vs. I, O vs. 0, 1 vs. I)

2. Readability

Can the reader understand and process text comfortably?

Determined by:

  • Line length (too wide = hard to follow)
  • Line spacing / leading (too tight = cramped, too loose = disconnected)
  • Paragraph spacing
  • Font choice (serifs for long text, sans-serif for web)
  • Alignment and white space

3. Expression

Does the typography convey the right tone and emotion?

Determined by:

  • Font personality (elegant, bold, playful, serious, technical)
  • Weight (bold = strength, light = refinement)
  • Color and contrast choices
  • Creative use of scale and hierarchy

Visual Hierarchy Pyramid

PRIMARY

Largest, boldest, most prominent — What viewers see FIRST

SECONDARY

Medium size, supporting role — What clarifies the primary

TERTIARY

Smallest, least prominent — Details & context

QUATERNARY — Fine print & legal

The 70-20-10 Rule: 70% of visual attention goes to primary element • 20% to secondary • 10% to tertiary. This creates instinctive understanding without viewers even thinking about it.

Key Takeaway

Typography is the most powerful design tool because letters are meaning. They carry both information and emotion. Master it, and you'll transform any poster, any design, any message into something that resonates.
