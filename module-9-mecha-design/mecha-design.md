### 1. Hard-Surface Design Principles
Unlike organic illustration, mecha design relies on clean edges and structural logic.

**Concepts Covered:**
- **Pivot Points & Hydraulics:** Designing joints that look like they can support tons of weight.
- **Panel Lining:** Using "cuts" and "seams" in armor to create a sense of scale and complexity.
- **Greebling:** Adding small, non-functional details (pipes, vents, bolts) to make a simple shape look like a complex machine.

---

### 2. Communicating Scale
How do you show that a robot is as big as a house?

**Concepts Covered:**
- **Focal Detail Distribution:** Placing tiny details near points of human interaction (like doors or cockpits) to establish a size reference.
- **Perspective Distortion:** Using dramatic 3-point perspective to make the machine loom over the viewer.
- **Texture Weathering:** Adding scratches, rust, and oil leaks to show the machine has been "used".

---

### 3. Industrial Grounding
The best sci-fi is grounded in real-world engineering.

**Concepts Covered:**
- **Reference Fusion:** Combining elements of modern fighter jets, heavy machinery, and medieval armor.
- **Material Logic:** Differentiating between heavy steel plating and lightweight composite materials through digital painting techniques.
- **Internal Mechanisms:** Thinking about what's "under the hood" to ensure the external design makes sense.
