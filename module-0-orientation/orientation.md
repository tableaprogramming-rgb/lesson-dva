Welcome to the Digital Visual Arts (DVA) course! This orientation serves as your roadmap for the semester, outlining our goals, grading systems, and the exciting projects ahead.

---

### 1. Welcome to DVA
Digital Visual Arts is more than just learning how to use software; it's about mastering the language of visual communication. Whether you're interested in graphic design, branding, or illustration, this course provides the foundational skills needed to create professional-grade assets.

### 2. What We Will Cover
The course is structured into three main phases:

#### **Prelim: The Foundations**
- **Fundamentals of Design:** Understanding the DNA of visuals (Anatome, Memento).
- **Poster Design:** Learning how to communicate complex messages through single compositions.

#### **Midterm: Application & Production**
- **Typography & Pattern Design:** The art of the letterform and the rhythm of repetition.
- **Packaging Design:** Moving from 2D designs to 3D physical prototypes.
- **Department Projects:** Real-world branding and event promotion for the SCS department.

#### **Final: Identity & Mastery**
- **Branding & Logo Design:** Creating cohesive brand identities from scratch.
- **Digital Illustration:** Drawing techniques, character design, and comic/manga styles.
- **Mecha Design:** Advanced mechanical design for robots and entities.

---

### 3. Grading Structure
Your performance is measured through two primary components:
- **Class Standing (70%):** This is the bulk of your grade, covering quizzes, performance tasks (your designs), and active participation.
- **Term Exams (30%):** Formal assessments at the end of each term.

---

### 4. Required Resources
- **Hardware:** A capable PC or laptop workstation provided in the lab.
- **Software:** We'll primarily focus on industry-standard tools for Raster (Adobe Photoshop, Affinity Photo) and Vector (Adobe Illustrator, Affinity Designer) graphics.

---

### 5. The Capstone: The Coffee Table Book
At the end of the semester, you won't just have a grade; you'll have a **Coffee Table Book**. This is a high-quality portfolio book containing your best works from the entire semester. This will be your primary takeaway for your professional portfolio.
