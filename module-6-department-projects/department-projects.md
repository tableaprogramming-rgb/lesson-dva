### 1. Merchandise Design (SCS T-Shirt)
Designing for wearables requires a different mindset than designing for paper.

**Concepts Covered:**
- **Economic Design:** Learning to work within "spot color" limitations (e.g., a 2-color maximum) to reduce printing costs.
- **The Human Canvas:** Understanding how designs wrap around the body and where the primary focus should be (usually the chest or back).
- **Semantics of Technology:** Choosing symbols that represent computing without falling into clichÃ©s (e.g., using abstract nodes instead of just "0101").

### 2. Event Marketing (SCS Week Poster)
The objective here is high-energy communication.

**Concepts Covered:**
- **Information Architecture:** Ensuring the "When" and "Where" are as prominent as the "What".
- **Color Palettes for Urgency:** Using bright accents (like the primary gradient we've learned) to catch attention in a busy hallway.
- **Brand Consistency:** Ensuring the poster feels like it belongs to the SCS, using established department colors and logos.

---

### 3. Midterm Outcome: The SCS Digital Gallery
The midterm concludes with a collaborative effort.

**Concepts Covered:**
- **Curation:** Selecting only the strongest pieces of work to represent the class.
- **Peer Review:** Developing a professional vocabulary for critiquing and improving each other's work.
- **Digital Presentation:** Organizing files into a virtual exhibition space (e.g., a multi-page PDF or a specialized gallery website).
